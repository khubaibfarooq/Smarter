How does a Proxies work?

When you use a VPN, it essentially replaces your internet service provider and handles routing your internet connection. Here's how.

1

A VPN connection links your device to a VPN server run by a VPN provider, like Proton VPN.

2

The connection between your device and the VPN server is encrypted, preventing your internet service provider from seeing the contents of your data as it travels between the two.

3

The VPN server sits between your internet service provider and the internet, blocking your ISP from seeing your online activity and preventing the websites you visit from seeing your real IP address.

4

When connected to a VPN server in another country, you can bypass local censorship and effectively “spoof” your geographic location.

What should you consider when choosing a VPN?

The most important thing to evaluate when choosing a VPN provider is whether it is trustworthy. This is crucial, as your VPN provider handles your internet connection, meaning it can see the browsing history you’re trying to keep private.

Here are some questions you should ask:
  • Does the VPN have a strict no-logs policy?
  • Is the VPN based in a country with strong privacy laws?
  • Is the VPN open source and independently audited?
Once you’re sure you can trust your VPN provider, you can consider other features based on what you need the VPN for, such as advanced security features, support for streaming, or ad blocking.
